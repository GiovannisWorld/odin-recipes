Braciole

Description

Braciole is a beloved traditional Italian dish that combines thin slices of tender beef, flavorful stuffing, and a rich tomato-based sauce. This hearty and comforting recipe has been passed down through generations, cherished for its robust taste and heartwarming aroma that fills the kitchen.

Ingredients

  • Thinly sliced beef (flank steak or top round)
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Minced garlic
  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Pine nuts
  • Raisins (optional)
  • Tomato sauce (marinara sauce or tomato gravy)
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Cooking twine
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the stuffing by combining breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped fresh parsley, pine nuts, and optional raisins in a bowl.
  2. Place the thinly sliced beef on a flat surface and spread the stuffing mixture evenly over each slice.
  3. Tightly roll up each slice of beef, enclosing the stuffing, and secure the rolls with cooking twine to hold their shape.
  4. Heat some extra-virgin olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Sear the braciole rolls in the skillet until they develop a golden-brown crust on all sides. This step helps lock in the flavors and juices.
  6. Lower the heat, and carefully add tomato sauce (marinara sauce or tomato gravy) to the skillet, enough to cover the braciole rolls.
  7. Cover the skillet and let the braciole simmer in the sauce for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and fully cooked.
  8. Once cooked, remove the cooking twine from the braciole rolls and discard them.
  9. Serve the braciole hot, accompanied by pasta (such as spaghetti or rigatoni) and topped with the simmered tomato sauce.
  10. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley for an extra burst of color and flavor.
  11. Enjoy your delicious and comforting Braciole with your loved ones!
